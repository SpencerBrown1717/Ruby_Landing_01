    Questions

    Fair questions, straight answers.

    Who pays Ruby?

    Employers pay placement fees, the way search has always worked. Concierge is an optional membership for candidates who want the full dashboard and an actively managed hunt. The Bench is free forever.

    Where does the data come from?

    Public hiring signals, filings, alumni networks, comp submissions from members, and Ruby's own placement history. It is pulled automatically and refreshed weekly, so you never open a spreadsheet.

    Is my search confidential?

    Yes. Nothing leaves your file without a yes from you first. Your dashboard is yours alone, and Ruby never markets your resume.

    What if I am not actively looking?

    That is exactly when to join the Bench. Ruby watches the market quietly, sends a quarterly pulse for your track, and reaches out only when something genuinely fits.

    Which markets does Ruby cover?

    New York City first: finance, insurance and risk, consulting, and executive and MBA program alumni. New cities open as members move.
